## Pass/No Credit (P/NC) Option

The P/NC option lets you explore a new discipline or take a genuinely
challenging course without the letter grade affecting your GPA.

- You may select **one course per semester** for P/NC, plus **one course in either Summer Session I or II** (Summer I and II count as one semester together).
- A passing grade converts to **P**; a failing grade (F submitted by the instructor) converts to **NC**.
- A **P does not help or hurt your GPA**. An **NC** also does not factor into GPA.
- No more than **21 credits of P/NC** may count toward the bachelor's degree.
- You must still attend the class after electing P/NC. Non-attendance can result in a **WU** grade, which is NOT converted to NC and DOES count in your GPA.
- P/NC selection is final after the deadline and cannot be changed.
- Elect or cancel P/NC through the P/NC Portal: https://apps.qc.cuny.edu/pnc/ (requires QC username and password).

### Summer 2026 P/NC & Unevaluated Withdrawal windows
- Summer I (June 1 – June 24): June 7 – June 16.
- Summer II 4-week (July 6 – July 29): July 12 – July 21.
- Summer II 7-week (June 29 – Aug 17): July 11 – August 3.

### Courses you generally CANNOT take P/NC (varies by catalog year)
- Any course in your major, major concentration, or a prerequisite for a major requirement.
- Most minor requirements (review with a faculty advisor).
- Graduate courses and Writing-Intensive courses (limits vary by matriculation year).
- For Fall 2015 and later matriculants: courses satisfying the QC Core (CUNY Pathways).
- English 110/120/120W, ACE Seminars, and basic-skills math (older catalogs).

> Important caution from the Advising Center: a "P" neither helps nor hurts your
> GPA, but an A or B you might have earned could have helped. If you plan to
> apply to graduate or professional school, check how they treat "P" grades
> before electing this option.

## Writing-Intensive ("W") courses

- "W" courses require 10–15 pages of evaluated writing across three or more assignments, attention to writing in class, essay exams if exams are given, and a max class size of 30.
- Recognize them by a course number followed by "W" (e.g., ENGL 152W).
- **Graduation requirement:** matriculants Fall 2013 and later must earn **2 W units**; those who entered Fall 1997–Spring 2013 need 3 W units.
- Up to two W units may transfer in, but **at least one W unit must be completed in residency at Queens College** for all students.
- Some courses are offered both with and without the "W" — pick the "W" section if you need the writing unit.

## Pathways / General Education (for Fall 2013 and later entrants)

- Students fulfill General Education by choosing from the offered Pathways course list.
- A course can satisfy **only one** Pathways requirement at a time, even if it appears under multiple categories.
- Structure: Required Core (College Writing 1 & 2, Math & Quantitative Reasoning, Life & Physical Sciences), Flexible Core (World Cultures, US Experience in its Diversity, Creative Expression, Individual & Society, Scientific World), and College Option (Literature, Language, Science, Synthesis).
- College Option courses must be completed in residency at QC unless completed at another four-year CUNY college — ask an advisor.
- Check prerequisites; you cannot earn credit for duplicative courses (e.g., a course with and without its lab component).
- Most current list: https://gened.commons.gc.cuny.edu/course-lists/

## Bursar / billing basics

- Your bill is on CUNYfirst Self-Service. Once you register, you assume financial responsibility.
- If you decide not to attend, you must drop your courses **before the first official day of the session** to avoid a financial obligation.
- If your balance isn't paid by your due date, you may be dropped from all courses.
- Liability after classes begin follows the refund schedule above (100% → 50% → 25% → 0% refund as the drop date gets later).
- All Bursar communications go to your QC email — check it regularly.
- Payment options: https://www.qc.cuny.edu/br/payment-options
